Robotic Pruning with Baxter Robot

Using the Baxter robot, my team created a robot capable of pruning plants in real-time. We utilized Robot Operating System (ROS) libraries to create the trajectory of bimanual arms and motion planning of Baxter robot. Additionally, the iterative design of end effectors (gripper and shears) were designed using SolidWorks with servo motors integrated that were controlled through Arduino. The real-time aspect of the project was accomplished using OpenCV using image processing techniques to detect patterns for leaves to prune. The end result was that our robot, successfully found four different orientations and created a trajectory of two arms to prune leaves in those orientations
